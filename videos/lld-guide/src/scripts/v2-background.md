## Scene: What & Why (~35s)
[Visual: SectionDiagram with highlightSection=1]
"The Background section captures the functional requirements and business context for the feature you're building. It answers two fundamental questions: what are we building, and why? This section feeds into every other part of the LLD. Your Data Models, Services, Flows, and Test Scenarios all trace back to what you define here. Get the Background right, and the rest follows naturally."

## Scene: Walkthrough (~80s)
[Visual: TableView with sub-section summary table]
"The Background has six sub-sections. Purpose describes the business problem being solved — focus on the 'why', not the 'how'. Functional Overview provides a high-level summary of what the feature does from an end-user perspective. Keep it concise — the detail comes in later sections. In Scope and Out of Scope set clear boundaries. In Scope lists what this LLD covers. Out of Scope explicitly states what it does not — this prevents scope creep and sets reviewer expectations. Assumptions capture known constraints or limitations that influence the design. These may need revisiting as the design progresses. Dependencies list external services, libraries, infrastructure, or teams your feature depends on, including their type, owner, and current status. Finally, References point to source documents like BRDs, HLDs, or epics — link to them rather than duplicating their content."
